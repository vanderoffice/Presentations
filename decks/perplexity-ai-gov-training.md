Perplexity is an **AI-powered research engine** that searches the internet and delivers answers with **cited sources** — so you can verify every claim.

:::collapse How is this different from ChatGPT?
ChatGPT generates answers from its training data — it can be creative but may "hallucinate" facts. **Perplexity searches the live web** for every query and cites real sources. You can click any citation to verify the original page.

| Feature | ChatGPT | Perplexity |
|---------|---------|------------|
| Live web search | Optional (plugin) | Every query |
| Source citations | Rare | Every claim |
| Real-time data | Training cutoff | Always current |
| Best for | Creative writing, coding | Research, fact-finding |
:::

## Writing Better Prompts

The quality of your answer depends on the quality of your question. A little effort upfront saves significant time.

:::tabs
## Effective Prompts
Good prompts are **specific**, provide **context**, and state what you need:

- "What are the current CalHR telework policies for state employees, and how have they changed since 2024?"
- "Compare California's water recycling regulations with Arizona's. Focus on agricultural reuse."
- "Summarize the key provisions of Executive Order N-30-25 regarding state government innovation."

**Pattern:** *[What you want] + [specific topic] + [constraints or focus area]*

## What to Avoid
Vague prompts get vague answers:

- "Tell me about telework" → Too broad. Which state? Which policies?
- "Water regulations" → A book could be written. What aspect? Which jurisdiction?
- "Innovation fellowship" → Which one? What about it?

**Why they fail:** The AI has to guess what you care about, and it usually guesses wrong.

## Voice Input
Click the **microphone icon** in the search bar to speak your question. Voice works well for:

- Quick questions while multitasking
- Complex questions that are easier to say than type
- Accessibility needs

> **Tip:** Speak naturally — you don't need special syntax. "What are the latest CalPERS retirement benefit changes?" works perfectly.
:::

:::collapse Example Prompts for Government Work

**Policy Research**
- "What are California's current requirements for state agency AI use under the Governor's executive orders?"
- "Summarize SB 1047 and its implications for state technology procurement."

**Data Lookup**
- "What is California's current unemployment rate compared to the national average?"
- "How much did California spend on Medi-Cal in fiscal year 2024-25?"

**Comparative Analysis**
- "Compare California's approach to homelessness funding with New York's. Include dollar amounts."
- "What are the pros and cons of centralized vs. decentralized IT governance in state government?"

**Document Analysis**
- Upload a PDF and ask: "Summarize the key action items from this meeting agenda."
- Upload a report and ask: "What are the 3 most significant findings in this audit?"
:::

## Deep Research

Deep Research is Perplexity's most powerful feature. It spends **up to 3 minutes** doing multi-step research — reading dozens of sources, cross-referencing facts, and building a comprehensive report.

:::columns

| | Regular Search | Deep Research |
|--|---------------|---------------|
| **Speed** | 10-30 seconds | Up to 3 minutes |
| **Daily Quota** | 300+ searches | Limited (resets midnight) |
| **Best For** | Quick lookups | Policy analysis, reports |
| **Sources Read** | ~5-10 pages | 20-50+ pages |

**How to access:** Click the **+ button** in the search bar, then select **Deep Research**.

> **Tip:** Use Deep Research when you'd normally spend 1-2 hours reading multiple sources. Use regular search for quick questions.

## Tips for Government Work

**Start every session with a clear question.** The more specific your prompt, the better the answer. Include agency names, policy numbers, date ranges, and what format you want the answer in.

**Always verify citations.** Perplexity cites its sources — click them. For anything going into official documents, verify the original source says what Perplexity claims it says.

**Use Deep Research for complex analysis.** If you'd normally spend an hour reading multiple sources, Deep Research can do it in 3 minutes. Perfect for legislative analysis, policy comparisons, and background research.

**Create Spaces for ongoing projects.** Don't start from scratch every session. A Space remembers your uploaded files and custom instructions across conversations.

**Know the limitations:**
- Perplexity cannot access your agency's **intranet** or internal systems
- It may not have access to **paywalled** academic journals or proprietary databases
- Answers should **complement**, not replace, official research processes
- AI can make mistakes — always apply professional judgment

:::collapse Common Pitfalls to Avoid
- **Trusting without verifying** — Citations exist for a reason. Click them.
- **Uploading sensitive data** — Keep PII, PHI, and confidential documents off cloud AI tools.
- **Using AI output verbatim** — Rephrase and verify before including in official communications.
- **Forgetting context** — Perplexity doesn't know your agency's internal policies unless you tell it.
- **Ignoring Deep Research** — Many Fellows only use basic search and miss the most powerful feature.
:::
